Research & Development in Transportation Engineering

ML-Based Prediction of Marshall Stability

Evaluated Sugarcane Bagasse Ash and Reclaimed Aggregates as sustainable alternatives in asphalt concrete and built ML models to predict Marshall Stability.

  • Collected experimental data from Marshall Stability tests.
  • Analyzed data from 72 specimens with varying SCBA (0–5%) and bitumen levels.
  • Developed Support Vector Machine achieving 99% training accuracy (R^2=0.99).
  • Identified 5% SCBA as the optimal replacement for maximum load capacity.

Outcome: Demonstrated potential for sustainable pavements using waste-modified asphalt mixtures.

Graph from B.Tech Project: Predicted Marshall Stability vs. Actual Lab Results.

Trip Generation Modeling: Neural Networks vs Linear Regression

Comparative analysis to predict household travel behavior and evaluate performance differences between traditional regression and neural network models.

  • Developed comparative models to predict household travel behavior using ML techniques.
  • Achieved 4.8x improvement in predictive accuracy (R² = 94.2%) over traditional methods.
  • Analyzed 1,800+ household records with comprehensive data preprocessing and feature engineering.
  • Identified household characteristics as primary trip generation drivers.

Outcome: Demonstrated substantial accuracy gains using MLP neural networks for trip generation forecasting.
